  1. Did ISU have better defense against West Virginia or Kansas State? Answer this question by creating a scatterplot with x-axis being the number of solo tackles (by each player) against West Virginia, and y-axis being that against Kansas State. A large number of solo tackles indicates good defense.

    (Hint: From step 1 we have gathered our defClean into 4 columns.
    Now we only want to focus on solo tackles (hintfilter). Look at your data structure. What form do we need our data to have in order to easily answer this question? (hintspread)).

defClean %>%
  filter(Statistic=="Tackles_Solo") %>% 
  spread(key=Opponent_Opponent, value=Value) %>%
  rename(KansasState = "Kansas State", WestVirginia = "West Virginia") %>%
  drop_na(KansasState, WestVirginia) %>%
  ggplot(aes(x=WestVirginia, y=KansasState)) + geom_point() + labs(title="Defensive Tackles: West Virginia vs. Kansas State", x="West Virginia", y="Kansas State") + xlim(0,9) + ylim(0,9)

From the plot, we can say that ISU has better defense against Kansas State than West Virginia.
